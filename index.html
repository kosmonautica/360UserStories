How to Play

Based on “360 Stories” by Wilma Mulder (www.360stories.one), adapted by Udo Wiegärtner for job situations in an agile context.

Purpose

  • Helps people share stories about their careers and work situations.
  • Personal, meaningful, and appreciative – no one-upping each other.

How to play – three steps, one after another

  • Step 1 – Direction (Wheel 1): The next storyteller spins the big wheel with their eyes closed. Someone else calls “Stop!”. The arrow points to up to four possible topics (one per ring).
  • Step 2 – Color (Wheel 2): The color wheel decides which of the four topics you'll tell a story about – the color indicates the ring.
    • Black = Blackbox: Your co-players pick one of the four topics for you.
    • White = Wildcard: You pick one of the four topics yourself.
  • Step 3 – Category (Wheel 3): Decides what kind of story is wanted:
    • First – What do you connect with the very first time in this context?
    • Last – What do you connect with the most recent time?
    • Best – What was a great moment in this context?
    • Worst – What was a tough moment in this context?
    • Advise – What advice would you give on this topic?
    • ? – Your choice: you pick one of the five kinds yourself – tap it.
  • Stories usually take 2–3 minutes.

A few tips for storytelling

  • Don't comment on stories – just listen.
  • No follow-up questions.
  • No “Oh, something similar happened to me…” – give the story the space it deserves.

No winning, no ending

  • There's no goal, no finish line.
  • Play as long as people want to share.
  • After each story: simply say “Thanks for sharing” – and the next person goes.
